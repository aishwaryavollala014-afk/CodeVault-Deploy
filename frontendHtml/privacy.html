Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026
CodeVault only ever accesses your own data, with your explicit authorization, and only to power your dashboard and your GitHub sync.

What we collect

  • Your GitHub identity (via OAuth) — used to sign you in and push to repositories you own.
  • Public statistics from the coding platforms you connect (solved counts, difficulty, topics, ratings).
  • For platforms you authorize for code sync: your own accepted submissions and their source code.

What we don't do

  • We never ask for or store your coding-platform passwords.
  • We never access other users' private submissions.
  • We don't sell your data or use it for advertising.

Your control

You can disconnect any platform or delete your account at any time from Settings. Disconnecting stops all future syncing immediately. Repositories already pushed to your GitHub remain yours.

Storage & security

Authorization tokens are encrypted at rest and used only to fetch your own data. Sessions expire and you are prompted to reconnect rather than us holding long-lived credentials.

Contact

Questions about privacy? Reach us via the contact page.
